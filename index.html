Advocacy & Community

Standing for Rights, Ending Stigma

Advocacy, awareness campaigns, legal rights, and community engagement for people living with HIV in Pakistan.

01

World AIDS Day — Pakistan

Annual December 1st campaigns coordinated with NACP, provincial health departments, and civil society organisations across major cities.

02

Anti-Discrimination Rights

People living with HIV are protected under Pakistan's National Health Policy. Employment, healthcare, and education discrimination is illegal and challengeable.

03

Stigma Reduction Programmes

Community-level education initiatives targeting healthcare workers, religious leaders, and school educators to reduce HIV-related stigma.

04

Key Populations Outreach

Targeted harm reduction services, counselling, and ART linkage for vulnerable populations, delivered through trusted community networks.

05

Media & Awareness Campaigns

National radio, television, and digital campaigns delivering HIV awareness to rural and urban populations in Urdu, Sindhi, Pashto, and Balochi.

06

Community Peer Support

Peer-led support networks connecting newly diagnosed individuals with trained community members who share lived experience and provide guidance.

Know Your Legal Rights

Pakistan's National Health Policy mandates confidentiality, equal access to healthcare, and protection from discrimination for all people living with HIV. If your rights have been violated, organisations like HRCP and Legal Aid Pakistan can help.
